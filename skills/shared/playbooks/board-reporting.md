# Board Reporting Playbook

Use this playbook when preparing board meeting materials — to structure the narrative, select the right metrics, frame decisions for board input, and communicate company performance with clarity and candor.

## When to Use

- You are preparing for a quarterly or monthly board meeting
- You need to structure a board deck or written update
- You need to decide what to highlight, what to escalate, and what to ask the board for
- You need to communicate bad news or a course correction to the board

## Process

### 1. Set the Narrative Arc

Every board update should tell a coherent story:

- Where were we last time? (prior commitments and outcomes)
- Where are we now? (current performance against plan)
- What changed? (wins, misses, surprises, market shifts)
- Where are we going? (updated forecast and plan)
- What do we need from the board? (decisions, input, introductions, approval)

### 2. Select the Right Metrics

Choose metrics that tell the true story — not just the good ones:

**Company-level (always include):**
- Revenue vs. plan and prior period
- Cash and runway (or profitability)
- Key growth metric (users, customers, ARR, GMV)
- Burn rate and efficiency metric (Rule of 40, CAC payback, magic number)

**Functional highlights (pick 2-3 per function):**
- Product: shipping velocity, adoption, key feature usage
- Sales: pipeline, win rate, quota attainment, average deal size
- Marketing: lead volume, CAC, channel efficiency
- Customer: NRR, GRR, NPS, churn rate, expansion
- People: headcount, key hires, attrition, engagement

**Red/green status:**
- Every metric should have a target and a clear green/yellow/red status
- Do not hide red metrics — explain them and the corrective action

### 3. Structure the Board Deck

Standard board deck structure:

1. **Executive Summary** (1 slide) — the narrative in 5 bullets
2. **KPI Dashboard** (1 slide) — all key metrics with targets and status
3. **Financials** (1-2 slides) — P&L summary, cash, runway, variance to plan
4. **Business Highlights** (1-2 slides) — wins, launches, partnerships, customer logos
5. **Business Lowlights / Challenges** (1 slide) — misses, risks, corrective actions
6. **Functional Deep Dives** (1 slide each) — product, sales, marketing, customer, people
7. **Strategic Discussion** (1 slide) — the one big topic for board input
8. **Asks and Decisions** (1 slide) — what you need from the board
9. **Appendix** — supporting data, detailed metrics, backup slides

### 4. Frame Decisions for Board Input

When you need the board to decide or advise:

- What is the decision?
- What are the options?
- What is your recommendation and why?
- What are the tradeoffs?
- What happens if we defer the decision?
- What information do you need from the board?

### 5. Communicate Bad News

- Lead with the fact, not the context
- Take ownership — do not blame external factors or team members
- Explain what you are doing about it
- State what you need from the board (if anything)
- Provide a timeline for resolution or the next update

Bad news delivered early with a plan builds trust. Bad news delivered late or without a plan destroys it.

### 6. Prepare for Board Q&A

- Anticipate the top 5 questions the board will ask
- Prepare backup slides with supporting data
- Know your numbers cold — board members will probe specifics
- Assign owners for questions outside your expertise
- Never bluff — "I don't know, but I'll follow up by [date]" is the right answer

## Output Pattern

```text
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
- 5-bullet narrative: last quarter, now, what changed, outlook, asks

KPI DASHBOARD
- All key metrics with targets, actuals, and status

FINANCIAL SUMMARY
- Revenue, cash, runway, burn, variance to plan

HIGHLIGHTS AND LOWLIGHTS
- Wins and misses with corrective actions

STRATEGIC DISCUSSION
- The one big topic for board input

ASKS AND DECISIONS
- What you need from the board, options, recommendation
```
