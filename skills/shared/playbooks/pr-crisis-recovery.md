# PR Crisis Recovery Playbook

Use this playbook when reputation damage, media scrutiny, or public backlash becomes decision-critical.

## When to Use

- A negative story, scandal, or backlash is spreading publicly
- Customers, employees, investors, or partners are losing trust
- You need a recovery narrative and response posture
- You need channel-specific messaging and trust repair actions

## Process

### 1. Assess the Damage

- What triggered the crisis?
- Which channels are driving the narrative?
- Who is shaping public perception?
- What is the reach, sentiment, and stakeholder impact?
- Is the core accusation true, partially true, false, or misunderstood?

### 2. Classify the Crisis Type

- Performance failure
- Ethical violation
- Misleading communication
- Leadership misconduct
- Data or privacy breach
- Cultural insensitivity
- Third-party association
- Misinformation

### 3. Choose Response Posture

- Full accountability
- Partial accountability
- Clarification
- Firm denial
- Strategic silence for low-signal issues that would grow if amplified

### 4. Build the Message

- Acknowledge what happened
- Apologize where warranted
- State what action is being taken
- Tailor messages for employees, customers, investors, partners, and media

### 5. Rebuild Trust

- Pair messaging with visible action
- Define proof points, checkpoints, and behavior changes
- Track whether sentiment and stakeholder confidence actually improve

## Output Pattern

```text
PR CRISIS SNAPSHOT
- Trigger, spread, sentiment, validity

RESPONSE POSTURE
- Accountability, clarification, or denial

STAKEHOLDER PLAN
- Who hears what, where, and when

RECOVERY ACTIONS
- Operational and reputational proof points
```
