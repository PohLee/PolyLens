# Crisis Management Playbook

Use this playbook during active crises or when testing crisis readiness.

## When to Use

- An operational, financial, leadership, security, or systemic crisis is unfolding
- You need to establish command and response cadence fast
- You need structured containment and continuity planning
- You need a recovery path after immediate stabilization

## Process

### 1. Assess and Classify

- What is happening right now?
- What systems, teams, customers, or locations are affected?
- Is anyone in danger?
- Is the issue contained or spreading?
- What crisis level best fits the scope?

### 2. Establish Command

- Name a single incident commander
- Assign operations, communications, technical, legal, people, and continuity leads
- Set an update cadence and logging responsibility

### 3. Contain the Damage

- Stop the bleeding
- Isolate affected areas
- Activate backups or degraded-mode operations
- Preserve evidence for later investigation

### 4. Stabilize and Plan Recovery

- Identify the current best understanding of cause
- Define the next 2, 8, and 24-hour actions
- Protect critical stakeholders and essential operations
- Escalate to deeper RCA and risk review as needed

### 5. Communicate Relentlessly

- Internal audiences need clarity on status and actions
- External audiences need timely, factual updates
- Silence creates rumor, drift, and secondary damage

## Output Pattern

```text
CRISIS STATUS
- Severity, scope, safety

COMMAND STRUCTURE
- Named owner and leads

IMMEDIATE ACTIONS
- Containment
- Communications
- Continuity steps

NEXT CHECKPOINTS
- 2h, 8h, 24h plan
```
