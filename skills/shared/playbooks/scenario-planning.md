# Scenario Planning Playbook

Use this playbook when a decision involves high uncertainty and a single forecast is not enough — to explore multiple plausible futures and build strategies that work across them.

## When to Use

- The future is highly uncertain and the cost of being wrong is high
- You need to stress-test a strategy against multiple futures
- You are planning for a 12-36 month horizon with significant unknowns
- You need to identify early warning signals for different outcomes

## Process

### 1. Identify the Focal Question

- What decision needs to be made?
- What is the planning horizon?
- What would make this decision regrettable in hindsight?

### 2. Map Key Driving Forces

List the forces that will shape the future:

- Market and customer trends
- Technology shifts
- Regulatory and policy changes
- Competitive dynamics
- Economic and macro conditions
- Internal capabilities and constraints

For each force, rate: importance to the outcome (high/medium/low) and uncertainty (high/medium/low). Focus on forces that are both high importance and high uncertainty.

### 3. Define Critical Uncertainties

Select the 2 most important and uncertain forces. These become the axes of your scenario matrix:

- Axis X: one critical uncertainty (e.g., "regulation: light vs. heavy")
- Axis Y: another critical uncertainty (e.g., "adoption: slow vs. fast")

### 4. Build Four Scenarios

The 2x2 matrix produces four distinct futures:

| | Axis Y: Outcome A | Axis Y: Outcome B |
|---|---|---|
| **Axis X: Outcome 1** | Scenario 1 | Scenario 2 |
| **Axis X: Outcome 2** | Scenario 3 | Scenario 4 |

For each scenario, describe:

- A memorable name (not "Scenario 1")
- The narrative: what does this world look like?
- Key assumptions that must be true
- Winners and losers in this scenario
- Implications for the focal decision

### 5. Test Strategies Against Scenarios

For each strategic option:

- How does it perform in each scenario? (strong / adequate / poor)
- Is there a strategy that performs adequately across all four? (robust strategy)
- What bets are scenario-specific? (contingent strategies)
- What are the no-regret moves that help in every scenario?

### 6. Define Early Warning Signals

For each scenario, identify leading indicators that signal it is becoming reality:

- What data points would tell us this scenario is unfolding?
- What is the trigger point for activating a contingent strategy?
- How much lead time do we have between signal and impact?

## Output Pattern

```text
FOCAL QUESTION
- Decision and planning horizon

KEY DRIVING FORCES
- Forces ranked by importance and uncertainty

SCENARIO MATRIX
- Two critical uncertainties as axes
- Four named scenarios with narratives

STRATEGY TESTING
- How each option performs across scenarios
- Robust strategies, contingent bets, no-regret moves

EARLY WARNING SIGNALS
- Indicators per scenario and trigger points
```
