# Competitive Analysis Playbook

Use this playbook when a decision depends on understanding the competitive landscape — positioning, differentiation, threats, or market gaps.

## When to Use

- You are entering a market, launching a product, or changing pricing
- You need to understand why you win or lose deals
- You need to identify competitive threats or white-space opportunities
- You need to position messaging against specific competitors

## Process

### 1. Define the Competitive Set

- Who are the direct competitors (same problem, same audience)?
- Who are indirect competitors (different approach, same outcome)?
- Who are substitutes (manual process, status quo, DIY)?
- What is the total addressable market and each competitor's estimated share?

### 2. Map Competitor Profiles

For each top competitor (3-5), capture:

- Positioning and messaging
- Target customer and ICP
- Core features and differentiators
- Pricing model and approximate price point
- Strengths and weaknesses
- Recent moves (funding, launches, partnerships, leadership changes)
- Market perception (reviews, G2 ratings, analyst mentions)

### 3. Compare on Decision-Relevant Dimensions

Build a comparison across the dimensions that matter to buyers:

- Feature coverage and depth
- Ease of use and time-to-value
- Integration ecosystem
- Support and customer success quality
- Security and compliance posture
- Pricing transparency and total cost of ownership
- Brand trust and market momentum

### 4. Identify Your Position

- Where do you win decisively? (defensible advantage)
- Where do you compete adequately? (table stakes)
- Where do you lose consistently? (gap to address)
- What is your unique value proposition vs. each competitor?
- Are you competing on product, price, service, or ecosystem?

### 5. Assess Competitive Trajectory

- Which competitors are gaining or losing momentum?
- What capabilities are competitors building that could threaten you?
- Are there emerging competitors or business model disruptors?
- What is your 12-18 month competitive outlook if nothing changes?

## Output Pattern

```text
COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
- Market size and key players
- Your position and trajectory

COMPETITOR PROFILES (top 3-5)
- Positioning, pricing, strengths, weaknesses, recent moves

COMPARATIVE POSITIONING
- Where you win, compete, lose
- Unique value proposition

THREATS AND OPPORTUNITIES
- Competitive risks to monitor
- White-space opportunities

STRATEGIC IMPLICATIONS
- What this means for the decision at hand
```
