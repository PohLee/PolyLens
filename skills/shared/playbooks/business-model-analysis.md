# Business Model Analysis Playbook

Use this playbook when evaluating, designing, or stress-testing a business model — to understand how the company creates, delivers, and captures value, and whether the economics are sustainable.

## When to Use

- You are designing a new business model or revenue model
- You need to evaluate whether the current model is sustainable
- You are considering a pricing change or business model pivot
- You need to stress-test unit economics before scaling

## Process

### 1. Map the Business Model Canvas

Fill out all 9 blocks:

- **Customer segments:** Who do we create value for?
- **Value propositions:** What problem do we solve? What bundle of products/services?
- **Channels:** How do we reach customers?
- **Customer relationships:** How do we get, keep, and grow customers?
- **Revenue streams:** How does each segment pay? What is the pricing mechanism?
- **Key resources:** What assets are essential? (people, IP, infrastructure, capital)
- **Key activities:** What must we do exceptionally well?
- **Key partnerships:** Who do we depend on? What do they provide?
- **Cost structure:** What are the major cost drivers? Fixed vs. variable?

### 2. Analyze Unit Economics

For the primary revenue stream, calculate:

- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) = Total sales & marketing spend / New customers acquired
- Average Revenue Per User (ARPU) = Total revenue / Active customers
- Gross Margin = (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue
- Contribution Margin = Revenue - Variable costs per unit
- Customer Lifetime (L) = 1 / Monthly churn rate
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) = ARPU × Gross Margin × L
- LTV:CAC ratio (target: > 3:1 for SaaS, > 5:1 for marketplace)
- CAC Payback Period = CAC / (ARPU × Gross Margin) (target: < 12 months)

### 3. Evaluate Revenue Model Sustainability

- Is revenue recurring, transactional, or usage-based?
- What is the revenue concentration risk? (top 10 customers as % of revenue)
- How predictable is revenue? (renewal rate, expansion rate, churn rate)
- What are the leading indicators of revenue health? (pipeline, activation, engagement)
- Is the model scalable? (marginal cost per additional customer)

### 4. Stress-Test the Model

Test against adverse scenarios:

- What if CAC increases 50%?
- What if churn doubles?
- What if a competitor cuts prices by 30%?
- What if a key partnership ends?
- What if a major customer leaves?
- What is the break-even point? How much runway exists at current burn?

### 5. Identify Levers and Vulnerabilities

- **Growth levers:** What changes would accelerate growth? (pricing, channel, product, market)
- **Margin levers:** What changes would improve profitability? (COGS reduction, pricing increase, mix shift)
- **Vulnerabilities:** What single points of failure exist? (key customer, key channel, key partner, key person)
- **Moat:** What protects this model from competition? (network effects, switching costs, data advantage, brand)

## Output Pattern

```text
BUSINESS MODEL CANVAS
- 9-block summary

UNIT ECONOMICS
- CAC, LTV, LTV:CAC, payback period, gross margin, contribution margin

REVENUE MODEL HEALTH
- Predictability, concentration, scalability, leading indicators

STRESS TEST RESULTS
- Scenario outcomes and break-even analysis

LEVERS AND VULNERABILITIES
- Growth levers, margin levers, single points of failure, moat assessment
```
