# OKR Planning Playbook

Use this playbook when setting, aligning, or cascading Objectives and Key Results — to ensure goals are ambitious, measurable, connected to strategy, and achievable by the teams that own them.

## When to Use

- You are setting quarterly or annual goals
- You need to align team goals to company strategy
- You are reviewing or revising existing OKRs
- You need to cascade OKRs from company to team to individual level

## Process

### 1. Set the Strategic Context

- What is the company strategy or north star for this period?
- What are the 2-3 most important outcomes for the period?
- What constraints exist? (budget, headcount, timeline, dependencies)
- What did we learn from the previous period's OKRs?

### 2. Draft Objectives

Objectives should be:

- Qualitative and inspirational (not a metric)
- Ambitious but not absurd
- Time-bound (quarter or year)
- Owned by a specific team or leader
- Limited to 3-5 per level (company, team)

Format: "Verb + outcome + impact" — e.g., "Become the fastest onboarding experience in our category"

### 3. Define Key Results

Each Objective gets 2-4 Key Results. Key Results must be:

- Quantitative and measurable (a number with a target)
- Outcome-focused, not output-focused (measure impact, not activity)
- Realistic but stretching (70% achievement is a good OKR)
- Owned by someone who can influence the outcome

Format: "Metric from X to Y by [date]" — e.g., "Reduce time-to-first-value from 14 days to 3 days by end of Q2"

Avoid vanity metrics, activity metrics, and tasks disguised as KRs.

### 4. Check Alignment

- Do team OKRs roll up to company OKRs?
- Are there gaps where no team owns a company OKR?
- Are there conflicts where team OKRs pull in opposite directions?
- Are dependencies between teams identified and agreed?
- Does every OKR have a clear owner?

### 5. Define Checkpoints and Scoring

- Weekly: progress check-in (on track, at risk, off track)
- Mid-period: midpoint review — adjust if context has changed
- End of period: score each KR on a 0.0-1.0 scale
  - 0.0-0.3: missed significantly
  - 0.4-0.6: made progress but fell short
  - 0.7-0.8: hit the stretch goal (ideal range)
  - 0.9-1.0: goal was not ambitious enough

### 6. Cascade to Teams

For each company OKR:

- Which team(s) contribute to this Objective?
- What team-level OKR supports it?
- What are the team's Key Results?
- What cross-team dependencies exist?

## Output Pattern

```text
STRATEGIC CONTEXT
- Company strategy, constraints, prior learnings

COMPANY OKRs
- Objective 1: [statement]
  - KR 1: [metric from X to Y]
  - KR 2: [metric from X to Y]
- Objective 2: [statement]
  - ...

TEAM OKR ALIGNMENT
- Team → Company OKR mapping
- Dependencies and conflicts

SCORING AND CHECKPOINTS
- Weekly check-in cadence, mid-period review, end-of-period scoring rubric
```
