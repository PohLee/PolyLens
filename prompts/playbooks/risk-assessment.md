# Risk Assessment Playbook

Use this playbook when a decision needs explicit exposure mapping instead of vague concern lists.

## When to Use

- You need to identify risks before committing to a project or strategy
- You need to build or refresh a risk register
- You need to compare options with different downside profiles
- You need to communicate exposure and mitigation to stakeholders

## Process

### 1. Set the Risk Context

- What is being assessed?
- What objectives must succeed?
- What is the time horizon?
- What constraints and risk appetite apply?

### 2. Identify Risks Across Categories

- Strategic
- Operational
- Financial
- Technical
- Legal or regulatory
- Reputational
- People
- External or environmental

### 3. Score Each Risk

- Likelihood: 1-5
- Impact: 1-5
- Velocity: how quickly it materializes
- Optional detectability or controllability notes

### 4. Prioritize

- Highlight critical and high risks first
- Note interactions where multiple moderate risks can compound
- Distinguish accepted, mitigated, transferred, and avoided risks

### 5. Define Mitigations

- Preventive control
- Detection signal
- Contingency response
- Owner and decision checkpoint

## Output Pattern

```text
RISK SUMMARY
- Scope and appetite

TOP RISKS
- Risk, score, velocity, mitigation

GO / MODIFY / BLOCK IMPLICATION
- What the risk profile means for the decision
```
