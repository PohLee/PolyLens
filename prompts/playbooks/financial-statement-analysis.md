# Financial Statement Analysis Playbook

Use this playbook when a decision depends on financial health, operating performance, or capital efficiency.

## When to Use

- You need to evaluate income statements, balance sheets, or cash flow statements
- You need ratio analysis across profitability, liquidity, solvency, or efficiency
- You need period-over-period comparisons or peer benchmarking
- You need to identify financial red flags before committing to a plan

## Process

### 1. Gather and Normalize

- Confirm which statements and reporting periods are available
- Note currency, accounting standard, and company context
- Normalize obvious one-time items when they distort the core picture

### 2. Build the Core View

- Revenue, gross profit, operating income, net income
- Assets, liabilities, debt, equity, working capital
- Operating cash flow, investing cash flow, financing cash flow, free cash flow

### 3. Analyze Ratios

- Profitability: margins, ROA, ROE, ROIC
- Liquidity: current ratio, quick ratio, operating cash flow coverage
- Solvency: debt-to-equity, debt-to-assets, interest coverage
- Efficiency: asset turnover, receivables velocity, cash conversion cycle

### 4. Look for Trend Signals

- Growth acceleration or deceleration
- Margin compression or expansion
- Working capital deterioration
- Cash flow quality versus reported earnings
- Signs of balance-sheet stress or financing dependence

### 5. Translate to Decision Risk

- What strengths support the plan?
- What weaknesses constrain the plan?
- Which metrics are stable versus fragile?
- What needs monitoring before a GO decision?

## Output Pattern

```text
FINANCIAL HEALTH SNAPSHOT
- Profitability
- Liquidity
- Solvency
- Cash generation

KEY SIGNALS
- Strengths
- Red flags

DECISION IMPACT
- What the numbers allow or restrict
```
