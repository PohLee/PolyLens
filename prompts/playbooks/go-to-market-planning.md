# Go-to-Market Planning Playbook

Use this playbook when launching a product, feature, or entering a new market — to structure the GTM strategy, messaging, channels, and success metrics.

## When to Use

- Launching a new product, feature, or service
- Entering a new market segment, geography, or vertical
- Repositioning an existing product
- Designing or revising a sales or distribution motion

## Process

### 1. Define the Launch Thesis

- What are we launching and for whom?
- What problem does it solve and why now?
- What is the unique value proposition in one sentence?
- What does success look like in 30, 90, and 180 days?

### 2. Identify the Target Customer

- Who is the ideal customer profile (ICP) for this launch?
- What are their buying triggers and decision criteria?
- Who are the economic buyer, end user, and technical evaluator?
- What is the expected sales cycle length?

### 3. Choose the GTM Motion

Select the primary motion and supporting channels:

- Product-led growth (self-serve, freemium, free trial)
- Sales-led (outbound, inbound, enterprise sales)
- Partner-led (resellers, integrators, marketplaces)
- Community-led (open source, advocacy, content)
- Hybrid (combination with clear primary motion)

### 4. Craft the Messaging Framework

- Problem statement: the pain we solve
- Value proposition: the outcome we deliver
- Differentiators: why us vs. alternatives
- Proof points: data, customers, benchmarks that back the claim
- Objection handling: top 3 objections and responses
- Messaging by audience: different messages for buyer, user, evaluator

### 5. Plan the Launch Execution

- Pre-launch: beta customers, case studies, content, sales enablement
- Launch day: announcement, press, social, email, events
- Post-launch: follow-up campaigns, customer onboarding, feedback loop
- Channel plan: which channels, what content, who owns each
- Timeline: key milestones and dependencies

### 6. Define Success Metrics

- Awareness: reach, impressions, share of voice
- Engagement: website traffic, demo requests, sign-ups
- Conversion: trial-to-paid, win rate, pipeline generated
- Revenue: ARR/MRR impact, CAC, payback period
- Retention: activation rate, time-to-value, early churn

## Output Pattern

```text
LAUNCH THESIS
- What, for whom, why now, success definition

TARGET CUSTOMER
- ICP, buying committee, sales cycle

GTM MOTION
- Primary motion and channel mix

MESSAGING FRAMEWORK
- Problem, value prop, differentiators, proof points, objections

LAUNCH PLAN
- Pre-launch, launch, post-launch activities and timeline

SUCCESS METRICS
- Awareness, engagement, conversion, revenue, retention targets
```
